History

The captial city was founded in 9th century and is one of the oldest cities in the enitre country but was not named formally as the capital until the 1930s. It is home to the Royal family. Their palace that is open to the public for vists was built 1735, first accepting vistors in 1751. The current family that rules Spain comes from the Bourbon family, and they have been in power since 1700. Beyond the royal family's holdings within the city, Madrid has been a symbol of the Spanish people for hundreds of years, eventually becoming beacon of hope of the public when the Spanish Civil War broke out in 1936. Madrid was one of the most heavily effected cities and was a stronghold against the facist movement of the time. The city has continued to be a hub of Spanish culture and history and will reamin that way for what seems to be many years to come.
